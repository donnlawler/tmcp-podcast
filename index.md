<h2 id="about-the-show">About the Show</h2>

<div class="about-show-content">

<p>
The Minimum Commitment is a short-form podcast devoted to film theory and critical interpretation, offering rigorous yet approachable criticism for curious movie lovers. Designed for listeners interested in cinema and criticism, it presents focused analyses that distill complex ideas into a concise, engaging format.
</p>

<p>
Each episode runs approximately 10 to 15 minutes, with occasional longer installments when the material demands it. Rather than attempting broad overviews, the show concentrates on a single idea, scene, or film, unpacking it through clearly defined theoretical and critical frameworks.
</p>

<p>
The emphasis is on precision, interpretive depth, and clarity of argument, giving listeners a structured way to think more deeply about what they're seeing and hearing on screen.
</p>

<p>
Topics range from iconic blockbusters to cult favorites and horror classics. One episode might explore mythic structure and hero archetypes in Star Wars, while another dissects the unsettling sound design in Halloween and how it shapes audience emotion. Across genres and eras, the podcast treats film as a site of formal construction, cultural meaning, and analytical inquiry, inviting listeners to slow down, look closer, and develop a more nuanced vocabulary for understanding the movies they love.
</p>

</div>

<h2 id="about-donn">About Donn</h2>

<div class="about-donn-content">

<p>
Donn Lawler is a film scholar, audio engineer, educator, and lifelong student of cinema. With more than three decades of experience in sound production and a master’s degree in Film and Media Studies from Arizona State University, his work sits at the intersection of film criticism, theory, and storytelling.
</p>

<p>
His interests range from horror and science fiction to film noir, westerns, and contemporary cinema, with a particular focus on how films create meaning through image, sound, structure, and performance. Through The Minimum Commitment, he explores the ideas beneath the movies we love, translating film theory into accessible conversations for curious viewers.
</p>

<p>
For Donn, films are more than entertainment. They are expressions of who we are, what we fear, what we hope for, and how we make sense of the world around us.
</p>

<p>
When not writing, teaching, or producing podcasts, he can usually be found watching another film and asking why it works.
</p>

</div>
