## About the Show

The Minimum Commitment is a short-form podcast devoted to film theory and critical interpretation, offering rigorous yet approachable criticism for curious movie lovers. Designed for listeners interested in cinema and criticism, it presents focused analyses that distill complex ideas into a concise, engaging format.
Each episode runs approximately 10 to 15 minutes, with occasional longer installments when the material demands it. Rather than attempting broad overviews, the show concentrates on a single idea, scene, or film, unpacking it through clearly defined theoretical and critical frameworks. The emphasis is on precision, interpretive depth, and clarity of argument, giving listeners a structured way to think more deeply about what they’re seeing and hearing on screen.
Topics range from iconic blockbusters to cult favorites and horror classics. One episode might explore mythic structure and hero archetypes in Star Wars, while another dissects the unsettling sound design in Halloween and how it shapes audience emotion. Across genres and eras, the podcast treats film as a site of formal construction, cultural meaning, and analytical inquiry, inviting listeners to slow down, look closer, and develop a more nuanced vocabulary for understanding the movies they love.
